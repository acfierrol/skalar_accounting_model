Internal, confidential. Supersedes `skalar_cashflow_model_knowledge_base.tex` (March 2026).
Audience: AI agents and engineers operating, modeling, or auditing Skalar cashflows.
Convention: all formulas in ASCII. All money in USD. All dates per-deal timezone-agnostic calendar months unless stated.

## 1. Business context

Skalar Inc. finances the Sales & Marketing (S&M) spend of customer companies. Before each S&M Period, Skalar advances capital sized to the company's expected S&M spend. In exchange, Skalar receives a contractual share of the margin-adjusted revenue (Reference Income) generated by the cohort of customers acquired during that period, until a capped multiple of the advanced capital is returned.

This is structured legally as an **investment in receivables**, not a loan to the company:

- Skalar acts as **Arranger** on behalf of one or more **Investors** (Skalar itself can be, and initially is, the sole Investor).
- The customer company is the **Company**; its subsidiaries join as **Guarantors**.
- The asset is the **Invested Receivable**: the amounts owed to the Company by a **Reference Cohort** (the customers acquired in a funded S&M Period).
- Investors' recourse is to the performance of the cohort, secured by a perfected security interest (UCC-1) over the Invested Receivables and proceeds. The Company services the receivables and retains all customer-facing obligations (Retained Obligations).
- Each Investment Amount is treated as **debt for tax and accounting purposes**; the structure is not a partnership.

Skalar's capital is one internal pool plus one external facility. The internal pool is **fungible**: equity contributions and retained sharing merge into a single undifferentiated cash balance — "recycled shares" is a flow-of-funds narrative, not a ledger category. The external facility is provided by General Catalyst (GC), itself structured as a mirror-image Customer Investment Agreement in which Skalar is the Company and GC is the Arranger (Sections 11–12). Skalar therefore operates both sides of the same instrument simultaneously: committed funder downstream, funded company upstream.

### 1.1 Document stack (per deal)

| Instrument | Function |
|---|---|
| Customer Investment Agreement (CIA) | Master agreement. Covenants, security interest, cancellation events, definitions. Schedule A holds the deal-specific commercial terms (the negotiable parameters of Section 3). |
| Investment Request (IR) | One per S&M Period. Company offers the upcoming cohort; states Expected S&M Spend and Investment Amount. Countersignature by Arranger updates the Schedule of Investments. Max one IR per S&M Period. |
| Funding Adjustment (FA) | Documents a Spend Reconciliation (actual vs expected S&M spend) and updates the Schedule of Investments. |
| Disbursement Date Report (DDR) | Per period: Actual S&M Spend, Reference Income, sharing calculation per open cohort, with backup data. |
| S&M Period Funds Flow | Net cash calculation delivered before each settlement: (A) new funding due to Company − (B) sharing due to Arranger + (C) funding adjustments. Sign of total determines payment direction. |
| Master Guarantee | Guarantors' joint obligations. |

### 1.2 Roles in the cash chain

```
Company customers --pay subscriptions--> Company (Collections Account)
Company --sharing remittance--> Arranger (Skalar) --repayment--> GC + Skalar retention
Skalar (equity) + GC (loan) --funding--> Company (next period's S&M)
```

## 2. Core objects and notation

| Symbol | Definition |
|---|---|
| `D` | Set of active deals. `|D| <= 100`. |
| `d` | A deal (one Company under one CIA). |
| `T` | Global timeline in calendar months. |
| `closing_month(d)` | Calendar month in which `P(d,0)` starts. Always the 1st of a month. |
| `P(d,n)` | n-th S&M Period of deal `d`, `n >= 0`. Maps to `T` at `closing_month(d) + n * period_length(d)` months. |
| `F(d,n)` | Funding amount for period n = `funding_pct(d) * expected_sm_spend(d,n)`. Contract term: Investment Amount. |
| `C(d,i)` | Cohort of customers acquired in `P(d,i)`. Contract term: Reference Cohort. Membership: customer's **first transaction** falls in `P(d,i)`. |
| `collections(d,k,i)` | Cash collected during `P(d,k)` from customers of `C(d,i)`. May be negative (refunds, credit notes, reversals). |
| `R(d,k,i)` | Reference Income = `collections(d,k,i) * gross_margin(d)`. |
| `S(d,k,i)` | Theoretical sharing = `R(d,k,i) * sharing_pct_eff(d,i)`. |
| `S~(d,k,i)` | Sharing actually collected, truncated at the return cap (Section 7.4). All cashflow formulas use `S~`. |
| `A(d,n)` | Funding adjustment for `P(d,n)` from spend reconciliation. Sign convention in Section 6. |
| `rho(d)` | Deal invariant = `funding_pct(d) / sharing_pct(d)`. |
| `F_eff(d,n)` | Effective funding = `F(d,n) - A(d,n)`. |
| `Omega(d,n)` | Set of outstanding (open) cohorts of `d` at `P(d,n)`: cohorts born at `i <= n` that have not reached their return cap. Contract term: Open Reference Cohorts. |
| `age(d,i,k)` | Cohort age in S&M Periods = `k - i`. |
| `t*(d,i)` | Payback period of `C(d,i)` (Section 7.1). |
| `k*(d,i)` | Closure period of `C(d,i)` (Section 7.5). `k* >= t*`. |
| `n_j(t)` | Local period index of deal `d_j` for calendar month `t` = `(t - closing_month(d_j)) / period_length(d_j)`. Exists only if `t >= closing_month` and the division is integer. |

**Index convention.** `i` always denotes a cohort's birth period. `i in Omega(d,n)` abbreviates "indices i such that `C(d,i) in Omega(d,n)`".

**Cohort assignment integrity.** Each customer's `first_period` must be computed over the **complete, unfiltered** transaction dataset. Only after assignment, discard customers with `first_period < 0` (acquired pre-closing). Filtering payment rows before computing `first_period` causes data leakage: a pre-deal customer can falsely appear as new.

**Collections definition (operational).** Collections are derived from the Company's billing-system exports. Count rows of type Payment with status Success; subtract Refunds. The exact dataset, columns, and customer-ID fields are specified per deal in Schedule A.

## 3. Deal parameters

### 3.1 Universal constants

| Parameter | Typical value | Definition |
|---|---|---|
| `max_cohort_life` | 10 years | Hard stop after the end of a cohort's origin S&M Period. Sharing and outstanding exposure drop to 0 regardless of amounts collected. |
| `breach_sharing_pct` | 100% | Sharing applied to a cohort after a threshold breach. |
| `currency` | USD | All funding and remittances. |
| `business_day_rule` | precede | Amounts due on a non-Business Day are due the immediately **preceding** Business Day. |
| `default_rate` | e.g. 18%/yr, 360/30 basis | Interest on late amounts owed to Arranger/Investors. |

### 3.2 Negotiable parameters (per deal, set in Schedule A)

| # | Parameter | Definition |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | `funding_pct(d, n)` | % of Expected S&M Spend that Skalar funds. Contract: Investor Funding Percentage. **Negotiated as an interval** `[f_min(d), f_max(d)]` from which the company elects each period's rate; a single fixed rate is the degenerate interval f_min = f_max. Exactly one funding rate per Reference Cohort. The rate is the **company's election against EXPECTED spend** (via the IR), reconciled to realized spend by Funding Adjustment; an over-request later underspent is observationally indistinguishable from a lower elected rate. |
| 2 | `sharing_pct(d, i)` | % of Reference Income Skalar collects from cohort i. Contract: Investor Sharing Percentage. Negotiated as its **own** interval `[s_min(d), s_max(d)]` and resolved the same way as funding, **independently** of it: `sharing_pct = funding_pct` (rho = 1) is one possible election, not a structural identity. Exactly one sharing rate per Reference Cohort. |
| 3 | `gross_margin(d, i)` | Margin applied to Collections to produce Reference Income. Contract: Margin. **Resolved per cohort** (at each cohort's IR) and **may differ between cohorts** — the deal need not carry one margin for its whole life. Default: a cohort's margin is fixed for that cohort's life; electable alternative: a within-life schedule `g(d, i, m)`. Election and schedule resolved and stored per cohort. |
| 4 | `base_multiple(d)` | Minimum MOIC if payback occurs within `base_payback_months`. **Params 4–7 are the default return-pricing strategy (payback MOIC ladder); pricing is a strategy family — alternatives (flat multiple, performance-indexed schedules) plug into the same `return_cap = MOIC × F_eff` slot.** |
| 5 | `base_payback_months(d)` | Max payback age (months) earning `base_multiple`. |
| 6 | `multiple_step(d)` | MOIC increment per **month** of payback beyond `base_payback_months`. |
| 7 | `max_multiple(d)` | MOIC upper bound. |
| 8 | `commitment_amount(d)` | Total capital Skalar commits to the deal. Contract: Maximum Invested Amount. |
| 9 | `commitment_period(d)` | Months to deploy `commitment_amount`. Contract: Availability Period (may auto-extend at a Roll Date unless either party gives notice). |
| 10 | `review_trigger(d)` | Funded periods triggering extension review. |
| 11 | `extension_periods(d)` | Additional periods granted on extension. |
| 12 | `period_length(d)` | S&M Period length. Whole calendar months only. |
| 13 | `max_period_funding(d)` | Cap on Investment Amount for any single period. |
| 14 | `max_growth_obligation(d)` | Max period-over-period S&M Spend increase Skalar is obligated to fund (e.g., 20%). Larger jumps require consent. |
| 15 | `min_investment_pct(d)` | Floor: if Company requests less than `min_investment_pct * actual_sm_spend` for a period, it is **deemed** to have received an Investment Amount at that floor (anti-cherry-picking; sharing applies as if funded). |
| 16 | `winddown_threshold(d)` | % of trailing-3M Reference Income whose company-initiated cancellation triggers wind-down payment (Section 9.2). |
| 17 | `calc_window(d)` | Days after period end to compute actuals (spend, R, S, A). |
| 18 | `settlement_window(d)` | Days after calculation to settle net payments. |
| 19 | `ts_validity(d)`, `daca_deadline(d)`, `audit_deadline(d)`, `trial_period(d)` | Administrative: proposal validity, DACA signing deadline, audit delivery deadline, initial month-to-month trial without bilateral commitment. |
| 20 | `income_scope(d)` | Revenue streams counted in Collections: a single product line, or all streams net of discounts. Fixed at signature. |
| 21 | `fx_inception_rate(d, i)`, `fx_cap_multiplier(d)` | Optional FX strategy, negotiated per deal. **Default: all collections in USD, no FX mechanics.** Per cohort at IR: Cohort Inception Rate e0 (spot at request). Per deal: cap multiplier kappa (e.g., 1.20). Local Reference Income converts to USD at `R_usd = R_local / min(spot, kappa * e0)`. Depreciation within the cap passes through (slower USD repayment); appreciation accelerates; depreciation beyond the cap is borne by the company (must remit extra local currency to meet the USD obligation at the capped rate). |

**Timing is per-deal.** `calc_window` and `settlement_window` vary across CIAs. One observed contractual pattern: DDR due <= 1 calendar month after period end, disbursement 1 month after the DDR due date. Another: 14 + 14 days. The model treats both as instances of the same two parameters. Source of truth per deal = its CIA/Schedule A.

### 3.3 Threshold mechanics (per deal)

Threshold testing is a per-deal election among formalized mechanics. Every deal record must state its mechanic explicitly; the **canonical mechanic is the one indicated in the executed documents** for that deal.

**Common basis (all mechanics, non-electable).** The test ratio is always:

```
ratio numerator   = Reference Income the cohort is entitled to: R = collections * gross_margin
ratio denominator = Actual TOTAL S&M Spend of the cohort's ORIGIN period
                    (Skalar's funding + the company's own contribution = full spend;
                     Expected S&M Spend as fallback while actuals unavailable)

cum(d, i, m) = sum_{x=0..m} R(d, i+x, i) / actual_sm_spend(d, i)
inc(d, i, m) = R(d, i+m, i)              / actual_sm_spend(d, i)
```

Rejected basis — never valid under any executed document: received-sharing numerator (`collections * gross_margin * sharing_pct`) or Investment-Amount denominator (`funding_pct * spend`). Sharing received is the cap-tracking variable (Outstanding Investor Share), not a threshold input; remittance lag would also make it systematically stale.

**Mechanic I — Linear ladder (company default).**

```
req(m) = p_l                                   at each explicit checkpoint a_l
req(m) = p_last + (m - a_last) * p_inc         for m > a_last
test:    cum(d, i, m) >= req(m)                for every m
```

A series with negotiated initial points (the checkpoint grid) growing linearly at slope `p_inc` after the last checkpoint. Fixed at signature; surplus carries forward (a weak period is not a breach while cumulative stays on schedule); overperformance never penalized. Checkpoint grids are instance values, not structure — they need not be consecutive ages (e.g., consecutive early ages, or a coarser quarterly grid). Resolved grids live in the scenarios sandbox.

**Mechanic II — Per-period incremental delta (electable alternative).**

```
checkpoints:  cum(d, i, a_l) >= p_l          for each (a_l, p_l) in thresholds(d)
delta:        inc(d, i, m)  >= p_inc         for EVERY m >= a_inc
```

Equivalent to `cum(m) >= cum(m-1) + p_inc` **re-anchored to the actual prior cumulative each period**: no surplus credit; overshoot ratchets the next requirement up.

**Stringency relation.** Not totally ordered. Mechanic II is stricter whenever the cohort has overperformed (II requires `actual cum + p_inc`; I only `schedule`); I is stricter following waived/cured shortfalls (I's schedule never re-anchors down). Model both before electing.

**CAUTION — agreement-language alignment.** Where an executed agreement's incremental-delta wording ("incremental Reference Income … in any one of such S&M Periods", tested "on any date") reads as Mechanic II while the company default is Mechanic I, the elected mechanic must be reconciled with the agreement's words before execution; where they diverge, amend the agreement or record the documented deviation. Same check applies to every upstream IR.

**Timing election.** `threshold_timing(d)`: `any_day` (canonical — breach exists the moment a completed period's data shows failure; "on any day" in the executed agreement) or `period_end` (term-sheet style — tested only at each period-end calculation).

**Exit election.** `threshold_exit(d)`: `breakeven` (canonical — cohort exits testing once `cum >= 100%` of origin spend) or `return_cap` (term-sheet style — funded cohorts tested while Outstanding).

**Election record.** Per deal: `threshold_mechanic ∈ {I linear ladder (company default), II incremental, further strategies as negotiated}`, `threshold_timing ∈ {any_day (default), period_end}`, `threshold_exit ∈ {breakeven (default), return_cap}`. Any deviation must appear verbatim in that deal's Schedule A and in the deal register, and the agreement language must be verified to match the elected mechanic (see CAUTION above). Upstream (GC) tests are defined per Investment Request and may take any mechanic — record the mechanic per vintage at each IR countersign (Section 12.3(1) applies to the elected mechanics, compared at equivalent ages).

**Tested Cohorts** include open cohorts and **Legacy (Historical) Cohorts**: the customer groups of each of the 12 monthly periods preceding deal closing (6 upstream), computed as if the deal had been in effect; exit per `threshold_exit`.

**Breach consequences (all mechanics):**

- Skalar's funding commitment suspends at Skalar's discretion (passing all tests is a funding condition precedent).
- `sharing_pct` for the breaching cohort rises to `breach_sharing_pct` (100%) immediately, until cap or expiry.

## 4. Funding lifecycle (one period, one deal)

For `P(d,n)`, `n >= 0`:

1. **Investment Request.** Company delivers IR for `P(d,n)` stating Expected S&M Spend; `F(d,n) = funding_pct * expected_sm_spend(d,n)`. Arranger must accept within its commitment if conditions precedent hold (caps per Section 9.3, thresholds passing, no cancellation event, reps true, security interest perfected, prior payments current).
2. **Pre-period funding.** Company receives `F(d,n)` — standalone wire if `n in {0,1}`, embedded in the settlement netting of `P(d, n-delta)` if `n >= delta` (Section 5).
3. **Execution.** Company runs S&M during `P(d,n)`. Customers whose first transaction falls in `P(d,n)` form `C(d,n)`. All open cohorts generate collections.
4. **Calculation** (`calc_window` days post period-end): compute Actual S&M Spend, `R(d,n,i)` and `S~(d,n,i)` for all `i in Omega(d,n)`, adjustment `A(d,n)`, threshold tests. Deliverables: DDR, FA if needed, Funds Flow.
5. **Settlement** (`settlement_window` days after calculation): net amount of Section 5 is wired. Late amounts accrue at `default_rate`.

## 5. Settlement and netting

### 5.1 Settlement timing — operation, calculation, settlement

Three resolved lengths fix a deal's cash calendar: the **operation period** `L_op = period_length(d)` (whole calendar months — monthly `L_op=1`, quarterly `L_op=3`, ...), the **calculation period** `L_c = calc_window(d)` (operation-period end -> DDR), and the **settlement period** `L_s = settlement_window(d)` (DDR -> cash). The single dated settlement event is the **disbursement date**:

```
disbursement_date(d,n) = end_of_period(d,n) + calc_window(d) + settlement_window(d)
```

This is the source of truth for timing. Commercial "Net-N" names the receivable lag `N = calc_window + settlement_window` in days (Net 30/60/90); the cadence (monthly, quarterly, ...) names `L_op`. Both are negotiated parameters resolved at signature; no mechanic depends on a particular N or cadence.

### 5.2 Netting principle, and the ideal-case net formula

**Principle (both layers, from Skalar's perspective).** On any date, for a counterparty, sum every component falling due that date — inflows to Skalar positive, outflows negative; the **sign of the total sets the direction** (positive => Skalar receives, negative => Skalar pays), discharged as a single wire. Downstream this runs at each new cohort's IR (sharing owed from prior cohorts, plus adjustments then due, vs. the new Investment Request); upstream it nets remittances owed against the new vintage's PFA, plus adjustment shares. Aggregation never extinguishes a component.

The closed form below is the **ideal, fully-matched case** — all of a period's components sharing one date. It is an idealization, not routine operation: in practice only the components that actually coincide are aggregated, and anything settled on its own date (notably adjustments) is netted separately.

```
Net(d,n) = F(d, n+delta) - sum_{i in Omega(d,n)} S~(d,n,i) - A(d,n)   # ideal matched case

Net > 0  => Skalar pays Company
Net < 0  => Company pays Skalar
```

### 5.3 Income-due lag and the period offset delta

```
lambda(d) = ceil( (calc_window(d) + settlement_window(d)) / days_in_period(d) )   # income-due lag (periods)
delta(d)  = lambda(d) + 1                                                          # netting offset (periods)
```

Income earned at age `k` becomes due at age `k + lambda`; the `+1` in `delta` is the single period by which funding is staged ahead, so the settlement of `P(n)` lands with the funding wire of `P(n+delta)`. Monthly deals (`L_op=1`): Net-30 (`calc+settle ~ 28-30d`) -> `lambda=1, delta=2`; Net-60 (`~60d`) -> `lambda=2, delta=3`. A quarterly deal (`L_op=3 months`) on Net-90 -> `lambda=1, delta=2`. (Since `ceil(1+x) = 1+ceil(x)`, this equals the previous `ceil(1 + (calc+settle)/days_in_period)` form; it corrects the earlier text that read delta=2 for monthly Net-60.) Netting funding against sharing is contractually permitted (Arranger may reduce the next Investment Amount by sharing owed, or Company may remit only the excess).

### 5.4 Standalone wires

`F(d,0)` and `F(d,1)` (generally `F(d,0..delta-1)`) are standalone wires: no prior settlements exist to embed them. They are not exceptions — conceptually they are settlements of virtual periods `P(d,-delta) ... P(d,-1)` with zero sharing and zero adjustment, wired at the corresponding virtual settlement dates (just before their target periods start).

## 6. Spend reconciliation (Funding Adjustments)

Let `delta_spend(d,n) = actual_sm_spend(d,n) - expected_sm_spend(d,n)`.

### 6.1 Underspend (`delta_spend < 0`) — mandatory

```
A(d,n) = F(d,n) * |delta_spend(d,n)| / expected_sm_spend(d,n)    (A > 0)
```

Company must return `A` to Skalar (or it rolls into the next unfunded period's IR by mutual agreement). Restores `funding_pct_eff = funding_pct`, preserving `rho`.

### 6.2 Overspend (`delta_spend > 0`) — Skalar's discretion

- **Top-up:** `A(d,n) = -funding_pct(d) * delta_spend(d,n)` (`A < 0`). Skalar wires additional capital; `funding_pct_eff = funding_pct`, `sharing_pct_eff = sharing_pct`. (Equivalently the Arranger may instead reduce its funding percentage; both are forms of Funding Adjustment.)
- **No top-up (hold):** `A(d,n) = 0`. Funding unchanged; the effective funding rate falls to `funding_pct * expected/actual` and the effective sharing rate follows the §6.4 operational rule (defaults to the negotiated rate unless Skalar elects otherwise).

### 6.3 Sign convention

| Sign of A | Meaning | Flow | Obligation |
|---|---|---|---|
| `A > 0` | Underspend refund | Company -> Skalar | Mandatory |
| `A < 0` | Overspend top-up | Skalar -> Company | Discretionary |
| `A = 0` | No adjustment | — | — |

### 6.4 Effective funding and effective sharing

```
F_eff(d,n)            = F(d,n) - A(d,n)
funding_pct_eff(d,n)  = F_eff(d,n) / actual_sm_spend(d,n)
```

`sharing_pct_eff(d,n)` is fixed at the settlement of `P(d,n)` and never changes for `C(d,n)`.

**Operational rule (no Scenario I/II taxonomy).** The effective rate applied to `C(d,n)` is the rate Skalar requests at reconciliation, **defaulting to the negotiated rate**. Absent an election, adjustments restore the negotiated proportion:

```
funding_pct_eff(d,n)  = funding_pct(d)     # default; reduced only if Skalar elects to hold on overspend
sharing_pct_eff(d,n)  = sharing_pct(d)     # default
```

The platform asks for the effective percentage; absent an election it uses the negotiated `funding_pct(d)` / `sharing_pct(d)`.

| Case | F_eff | Effective rate (default) |
|---|---|---|
| `delta_spend = 0` | `F` | negotiated `funding_pct` / `sharing_pct` |
| Underspend (refund) | `< F` | negotiated (proportion restored) |
| Overspend + top-up | `> F` | negotiated (proportion restored) |
| Overspend, hold (`A=0`) | `= F` | `funding_pct * expected/actual` (Skalar's elected reduction) |

Rationale for the simplification: funding is elected against **expected** spend via the IR and reconciled to realized spend by a Funding Adjustment; an over-request that is later underspent is observationally indistinguishable from a lower elected rate, so a single rule (request the effective rate, default to negotiated) captures every case the former Scenario I/II split enumerated.

**Adjustments are not bound to the settlement calendar.** They may be received or paid outside the settlement window and are exercised as soon as actual spend is determinable — standalone cash events, not settlement components. Two reasons: pricing is MOIC-based (caps are multiples of `F_eff`, so effective funding must be corrected as fast as possible), and the GC ratable share (~95% of the flow) is treated upstream as a capital advance on the vintage. Adjustments can be exercised early and must be exercised diligently; one settled out-of-cycle never re-enters a later net.

**Adjustments are nominal.** No interest component, regardless of period length. For long periods (e.g., quarterly) Skalar carries excess/deficit capital longer than for monthly deals; accepted business decision, mitigated by portfolio cross-collateralization and by the fact that discretionary top-ups buy into already-materialized cohorts.

## 7. Returns: payback, MOIC, return cap

### 7.1 Payback

```
t*(d,i) = min { t >= i : sum_{k=i..t} S~(d,k,i) >= F_eff(d,i) }
```

Payback age in **months** (the MOIC input):

```
a*_months(d,i) = (t*(d,i) - i) * period_length(d)
```

The age is determined by the period being calculated, not by when the calculation runs: payback occurring in `P(d,t*)` has age `t* - i` periods even though confirmation happens in the following period's calculation window. Contractually the Payback Period is measured assuming sharing payments fall due on the final day of each S&M Period (ignoring calc/settlement lag), rounded up to whole months.

### 7.2 MOIC

```
MOIC(d,i) = min( base_multiple(d)
                 + max(0, a*_months(d,i) - base_payback_months(d)) * multiple_step(d),
                 max_multiple(d) )
```

Monotone non-decreasing in payback age, capped at `max_multiple`. (Concrete ladder parameters are scenario values; see the sandbox.)

### 7.3 Return cap

```
return_cap(d,i) = MOIC(d,i) * F_eff(d,i)
```

Contract terms: Return Multiple Cap / Investor Cap Amount. Forced to 0 at `max_cohort_life` after the origin period (10-year hard stop).

### 7.4 Sharing actually collected

```
S~(d,k,i) = min( S(d,k,i),
                 max(0, return_cap(d,i) - sum_{m=i..k-1} S~(d,m,i)) )
```

Recursive truncation: each period Skalar collects the lesser of theoretical sharing and the remaining distance to the cap. After the cap, `S~ = 0` automatically. The contractual mirror is the **Outstanding Investor Share**: `max(0, return_cap - cumulative remittances)`, deemed 0 after 10 years.

### 7.5 Cohort closure

```
k*(d,i) = min { k >= i : sum_{m=i..k} S~(d,m,i) >= return_cap(d,i) }
```

`k* >= t*`; equality possible. From `k*+1`, the cohort leaves `Omega(d)` and the Company retains 100% of its income.

### 7.6 Economics per cohort

```
nominal_gain(d,i) = return_cap(d,i) - F_eff(d,i) = F_eff(d,i) * (MOIC(d,i) - 1)

IRR r solves:  -F_eff(d,i) + sum_{k=i..k*} S~(d,k,i) / (1+r)^(k-i) = 0
```

## 8. Full flow chain (one cohort, one period)

```
collections(d,k,i)
  x gross_margin(d)            -> R(d,k,i)         [Reference Income]
  x sharing_pct_eff(d,i)       -> S(d,k,i)         [theoretical sharing]
  truncate at return_cap       -> S~(d,k,i)        [Skalar's effective sharing]
  x gc_sharing_pct(tau)        -> GC portion       [while vintage below gc_cap, Section 11.5]
  S~ - GC portion              -> Skalar retention
```

Cut-off conditions:

- Cohort past return cap or past `max_cohort_life`: `sharing_pct_eff = 0`; Company keeps 100%.
- Vintage past `gc_cap` (min of 2.0x and 16% XIRR) or past 10 years: `gc_sharing_pct = 0`; Skalar keeps 100% of its sharing.

## 9. Protective mechanics

### 9.1 Cancellation Events (per CIA)

Sale of Company without consent; insolvency proceedings; rep/warranty falsity likely causing a Material Adverse Change; regulatory event likely causing a MAC; acceleration/turbo provisions under any senior loan; covenant breach; Collections Account covenant breach. Consequences:

- Arranger may terminate further funding commitments. Company continues remitting sharing for all open cohorts until Outstanding Investor Share reaches 0.
- On fraud, embezzlement, gross malfeasance, or a Cancellation Event, the Arranger may **accelerate**: demand the full Outstanding Investor Share within 5 Business Days (reversible at Arranger discretion for curable events).

### 9.2 Wind-down

If the Company stops offering/renewing contracts (company-initiated, including involuntary app-store withdrawal) affecting more than `winddown_threshold(d)` of any open cohort's trailing-3-month Reference Income:

```
affected_proportion(d,i) = ref_income_cancelled_3M(d,i) / ref_income_total_3M(d,i)
outstanding_exposure(d,i) = return_cap(d,i) - sum_{k=i..t} S~(d,k,i)
winddown_payment(d,i) = outstanding_exposure(d,i) * affected_proportion(d,i)
```

Applies only to company-initiated cancellations, not end-customer churn, not customer default/credit deterioration, and **not** terminations from the Company enforcing its published safety, content, or acceptable-use policies (e.g., CSAM, imminent-harm removals) in the ordinary course.

### 9.3 Funding caps and floors

- Lifetime: cumulative Investment Amounts `<= commitment_amount(d)` without consent.
- Per period: `F(d,n) <= max_period_funding(d)` and consistent with at most `max_growth_obligation(d)` S&M growth vs the prior period.
- One Investment Request per S&M Period, max.
- Deemed minimum: if Company requests IRs totaling less than `min_investment_pct(d) * actual_sm_spend(d,n)`, it is deemed to have received that minimum Investment Amount (sharing obligations attach accordingly).
- Mutual no-funding notice: each party uses commercially reasonable efforts to give ~14 days' notice before a period if it will not request/fund.
- Arranger may permanently terminate future funding by notice (Arranger Termination Notice); existing cohorts unaffected.
- On a Sale of Company, the Company may cancel commitments and prepay remaining amounts up to the Investor Cap Amount on open cohorts (good-faith estimate of what would have been due), within a window around closing.

### 9.4 Company covenants relevant to the model (monitoring inputs)

- **Data access:** continuous (at least daily) ETL access to systems holding customers, cohorts, Reference Income, threshold calculations, Actual S&M Spend — including 12 months of pre-closing history (Historical Cohorts).
- **Monthly reporting (<= 30 days after month end):** unaudited financials; threshold test calculation per open cohort; raw billing exports.
- **Per settlement:** DDR (within `calc_window`), Funds Flow (>= 2 Business Days before disbursement).
- **Collections Account:** all cohort payments remitted into a designated account; Arranger has read-only visibility of all operating accounts (Monitored Accounts); no control rights.
- **Negative covenants:** no liens/sales on Invested Receivables (other than permitted); no competing receivable-based financings on the same income; no material amendments/extensions of receivables beyond de minimis caps without consent; new indebtedness requires intercreditor/subordination consent; subsidiaries must join as Guarantors; anti-cash-hoarding (subsidiaries must upstream Reference Income before payments fall due).
- S&M spend definition ties to the Company's income-statement marketing line; capitalized S&M is amortized in and disclosed via pro-forma statements.

## 10. Multi-deal portfolio aggregation

Deals are independent (own parameters, cohorts, cycle). Aggregation key = **calendar month `t`**, never local period indices (deals with different `period_length` have incomparable indices).

`D(t)` = deals with a period **starting** at `t` (i.e., `n_j(t)` exists).

```
funding_out(t)  = sum_{j in D(t+1)} F(d_j, n_j(t+1))      # capital staged in t for periods starting t+1
sharing_in(t)   = sum_{j in D(t)} sum_{i in Omega(d_j, n_j(t)-1)} S~(d_j, n_j(t)-1, i)
adjustments_in(t)  = sum_{j in D(t)} max(0,  A(d_j, n_j(t)-1))     # mandatory underspend refunds
adjustments_out(t) = sum_{j in D(t)} max(0, -A(d_j, n_j(t)-1))     # discretionary top-ups

net_flow(t) = sharing_in(t) + adjustments_in(t) - funding_out(t) - adjustments_out(t)
```

`net_flow(t) < 0` => Skalar covers the deficit with equity or the GC line.

Settlement of `P(d,n)` executes in the month where `P(d,n+1)` starts; the settled period index is `n_j(t) - 1`. For `period_length > 1`, `n_j(t-1)` does not exist — `n_j(t)-1` is the correct form.

**Net capital deployed** (capital "on the street" at month t):

```
net_capital(t) = sum_j sum_{k=0..n_j(t)} F_eff(d_j,k)
               - sum_j sum_{k=0..n_j(t)} sum_{i in Omega(d_j,k)} S~(d_j,k,i)
```

## 11. Upstream facility: GC Customer Investment Agreement

Source of truth: the GC–Skalar Customer Investment Agreement (the upstream facility). GC acts as Arranger on behalf of one or more senior Investors; Skalar is the **Company**. A separate Security Agreement applies; a Collections Account is required at closing, with a springing control agreement on request. (Investor identities and ownership splits, the effective date, governing law, and accounting elections are deal facts held in the scenarios sandbox, not here.)

The structure is a mirror image of Skalar's downstream CIAs. Translation of roles:

| Upstream term | Meaning |
|---|---|
| Underlying Investment Recipient | A Skalar portfolio company (e.g., a downstream CIA counterparty). Listed in Schedule D. |
| Underlying Product | The recipient's app/game. |
| Underlying Agreement | The downstream CIA between Skalar and the recipient. |
| Customer | An end-user of any Underlying Product. |
| Periodic Funding Amount (`PFA(tau)`) | The Investment Amount GC funds for vintage `tau`, post Funding Adjustments. |

### 11.1 Vintages (upstream Reference Cohorts)

Upstream S&M Periods are **calendar months from the facility's effective date** (the commencement date is a scenario value). The upstream Reference Cohort for month `tau` (a GC *vintage*) is the set of Customers **across all Underlying Products whose Join Date falls within `tau`** — keyed by customer join month, not by Skalar's funding month or any deal's local period index.

```
vintage(tau) = { customers c : join_month(c) = tau }   across all Underlying Products
```

Implementation columns: `join_month` (vintage key), `cohort_month` (downstream deal-cohort birth month), `sales_and_marketing_period_month`. For monthly downstream deals, `join_month = cohort_month`. For `period_length > 1`, one deal cohort smears across multiple GC vintages — attribute each customer individually by join month.

Upstream Legacy Reference Cohorts: the **6** monthly periods preceding the effective date (downstream deals use 12; do not conflate).

Upstream S&M Spend = Skalar's sales-and-marketing expenses tied to Underlying Products recognized on Skalar's income statement for the month — operationally, Skalar's funding deployments plus directly attributable spend.

### 11.2 Upstream Reference Income — accrual, greater-of

Upstream Reference Income for vintage `tau` in month `m` is the portion of underlying revenue **Skalar is actually entitled to** under the Underlying Agreements, with two contract-critical properties:

```
RI_gc(tau, m) = max( amount_due_per_Customer_Dataset(tau, m),
                     amount_actually_collected(tau, m) )
```

1. **Accrual:** it occurs when it becomes **earned and due** under the downstream CIA, not when collected.
2. **Greater-of:** if a recipient underpays, Skalar still owes GC on the contractual entitlement.

Consequence: Skalar bears 100% of portfolio collection risk vis-à-vis GC. Late or defaulted downstream remittances do not reduce or delay upstream obligations.

### 11.3 Funding cycle

- Skalar **may, but is not obligated to**, submit an Investment Request per upstream S&M Period (max one per period).
- GC acceptance is **fully discretionary** — no commitment, no Availability cap, no Maximum Invested Amount. Availability Period: 1 year, auto-rolling unless either party gives 30 days' notice before the Roll Date. Funding within 3 Business Days of acceptance.
- `gc_funding_pct(tau) = PFA(tau) / expected_sm_spend_skalar(tau)`, **capped at 0.95** absent GC consent. Skalar therefore always contributes `>= 5%` equity per vintage.
- All flows settle **net** (upstream §2.1(e)): new funding, Reference Income remittances, and Funding Adjustments offset in a single wire.

Funding identity per vintage — exactly two sources, GC money and Skalar money:

```
F_skalar(t+1) = gc_funded(t) + skalar_funded(t)

gc_funded(t)     = gc_funding_pct(t) * F_skalar(t+1)     # operated at the 0.95 ceiling, = PFA
skalar_funded(t) = (1 - gc_funding_pct(t)) * F_skalar(t+1)   # 0.05, drawn from the pool
```

**Leverage structure (per-vintage proportion invariant).** The senior/junior split — GC senior advance `gc_funding_pct` and Skalar junior co-investment `1 − gc_funding_pct`, currently **0.95 : 0.05** (≈19:1) — is the **leverage structure** of each vintage, the upstream analogue of the downstream `rho` invariant (Section 6). It is a parameter of the present regime, temporary: `gc_funding_pct` may be revised and the senior side may later be syndicated across several actors (then `gc_funding_pct` aggregates their advances). Skalar requests the `gc_funding_pct` ceiling on every IR as policy (leverage efficiency: every Skalar-side dollar carries 19 GC dollars), and the split is restored through Funding Adjustments after each spend reconciliation — GC participates ratably in top-ups and refunds, so post-FA `PFA(tau) / actual_sm_spend_skalar(tau) = 0.95` unless GC has documented an election to deviate.

**Single fungible pool.** All Skalar-side capital lives in one undifferentiated cash balance:

```
pool(t) = pool(t-1)
        + equity_contributions(t)
        + retained_sharing(t)          # (1 - gc_sharing_pct) * RI during repayment
                                       #  + 100% of RI on post-cap vintages
        + adjustments_in(t)
        - skalar_funded(t)
        - upstream_remittances(t)      # net of any wire-level netting (§2.1(e))
        - opex(t)
```

Dollars in the pool are indistinguishable by origin. "Recycling" describes a flow (sharing arrives, the pool funds the next vintage), not a capital tranche: no liability, claim, or formula may condition on whether a deployed dollar originated from an equity raise or from retained sharing. The only origin-tracking in the system is **per-vintage GC attribution** (`PFA(tau)`, dated remittances) — required by the upstream cap mechanics. Skalar-side capital has no vintage identity.

Fungibility is what makes the proportion invariant serviceable: the fixed 0.05 co-funding obligation on every vintage is met from whatever cash the pool holds, so retained sharing services new vintages at full 19:1 leverage instead of sitting as an idle tranche. Fungibility preserves the efficiency of the leverage; the invariant preserves its discipline.

GC's net wire `L(t)` is a settlement artifact, not a capital source distinct from `gc_funded`: new GC funding and amounts due to GC offset in one transfer (§2.1(e)). `L(0)`, `L(1)` are full standalone fundings (no prior remittances to net). Planning rule: GC is uncommitted, so `gc_funded(t)` is a **request** — the funding plan must remain solvent at `gc_funded(t) = 0` (Section 12).

Spend reconciliation upstream mirrors Section 6 with GC discretion on both legs: excess → GC may top-up or reduce its funding percentage; underspend → GC elects roll-forward or refund. GC participates ratably (`gc_funding_pct`) in downstream-driven adjustments per vintage.

### 11.4 Timing

```
upstream_DDR_due(tau)        = end_of_month(tau) + 14 calendar days
upstream_disbursement(tau)   = upstream_DDR_due(tau) + 3 calendar days    # ~day 17
extension                    = at GC discretion, max to last day of month tau+1
funds_flow                   >= 2 Business Days before each disbursement
```

Each monthly remittance covers the Reference Income that became due during that S&M Period across all open vintages. Compare the downstream settlement lag, which is template-dependent (often ~day 60): see Section 12.1.

### 11.5 Repayment waterfall and the cap

Per vintage `tau`: `gc_sharing_pct(tau) = gc_funding_pct(tau)` (i.e., ≤ 0.95; **1.00 immediately upon Cohort Threshold Breach** of that vintage).

The cap is the **lesser of two legs**, evaluated on dated cashflows at every calculation date:

```
gc_cap(tau, m) = min( 2.0 * PFA(tau),
                      xirr16_amount(tau, m) )

xirr16_amount(tau, m) = cumulative remittance level such that
                        XIRR( {-PFA(tau) @ funding date}
                              ∪ {+remittances @ actual dates} ) = 16%
                        as of the calculation date
```

XIRR semantics are contractual (spreadsheet XIRR; Arranger→Company flows negative, Company→Arranger positive). `gc_cap` is **time-increasing** up to the 2.0x ceiling (16%/yr accrual reaches 2.0x at ~4.7 years) and drops to zero 10 years after the vintage's S&M Period.

Monthly remittance per vintage:

```
remit(tau, m) = min( gc_sharing_pct(tau) * RI_gc(tau, m),
                     gc_cap(tau, m) - cum_remit(tau, m-1) )

gc_gross_repayment(m) = sum_{tau open} remit(tau, m)
```

In the closing month GC takes only the amount that exactly meets the cap; thereafter Skalar retains 100% of vintage income. Skalar retention before closing: `(1 - gc_sharing_pct(tau)) * RI_gc(tau, m)`.

### 11.6 Threshold tests upstream

The upstream Cohort Threshold Test is **defined in each Investment Request** — renegotiated monthly, not fixed in Schedule A, and may take any mechanic of Section 3.3 (record the election per vintage at countersign). Tested Cohorts: open vintages + the 6 upstream Legacy cohorts; a vintage exits testing once cumulative Reference Income exceeds its Actual S&M Spend (`breakeven` exit). Breach is "on any day" (`any_day` timing). Breach consequences: `gc_sharing_pct → 1.00` for that vintage immediately; future GC funding (already discretionary) effectively stops. Governance rule in Section 12.3.

### 11.7 Wind-down pass-up

On an **Underlying Investment Material Adverse Change** at any recipient (deterioration of the recipient's condition, of Skalar's ability to collect from it, or of enforceability):

```
wound_down_pct(tau) = RI attributable to the affected recipient over the prior
                      3 full S&M Periods / total vintage RI over the same window
trigger: wound_down_pct(tau) > 5%
payment(tau) = wound_down_pct(tau) * outstanding_gc_cap(tau)
```

Tested and due on each Disbursement Date Report. GC must act reasonably in extending the obligation while Skalar pursues recovery in good faith. Note the asymmetry with the downstream wind-down (Section 9.2): downstream triggers on company-initiated cancellations; upstream triggers on recipient MAC — including insolvency, where Skalar's downstream claim may be impaired while its upstream obligation is not.

### 11.8 Cancellation, acceleration, termination

Upstream Cancellation Events include all company-level events (Sale of Skalar without approval, Skalar insolvency, rep breach → MAC, Regulatory Event, senior loan acceleration, covenant breach) **plus recipient-level events**: insolvency of any active recipient, recipient rep breach under its Underlying Agreement → MAC, Regulatory Event at a recipient — each curable by applying the Wind-Down Provision.

- On a Cancellation Event, GC may stop further investments; Skalar continues remitting per vintage until each Outstanding Investor Share = 0.
- On fraud/embezzlement/gross malfeasance or a Cancellation Event: GC may accelerate the full aggregate Outstanding Investor Share, due within **3 Business Days** (reversible for curable events). Downstream mirror is 5 Business Days — see Section 12.3.
- **Early Termination Event** (Skalar's option): if GC or affiliates enter a CIA with a Disqualified Investor (a Skalar competitor) whose underlying recipient is in Central or South America, Skalar may terminate on 5 Business Days' notice by prepaying the aggregate Outstanding Investor Share for all open vintages.
- Default rate upstream: **16%/yr**, 360/30. Downstream template: 18%.
- Business-day convention: upstream §1.5 says preceding, §10 says succeeding (internal contradiction). Operate on **preceding** for Skalar payables.

### 11.9 Graduation and Direct CIAs

A recipient becomes **Graduated** when (a) its single-period S&M Spend exceeds $2,500,000 **and** (b) its aggregate Outstanding Investor Share / net exposure exceeds $20,000,000. GC may then, at its sole discretion, require Skalar to terminate/prepay the Underlying Agreement and enter a **Direct CIA** with that recipient; GC will use reasonable endeavours to convey up to **49% of each Direct CIA cohort origination** back to Skalar on negotiated sale terms. Structural cap on Skalar's upside in its largest deals — track both triggers per recipient (Section 12.4).

### 11.10 Capital disruption asymmetry

The Capital Disruption protection of Section 9.3 / downstream CIAs (notice + 60-day cure + penalty-free commitment termination) exists only **downstream** — it is what Skalar grants its companies. The GC CIA contains **no equivalent protection for Skalar**: GC funding is discretionary at all times. The downstream Capital Disruption clause is therefore Skalar's only contractual relief valve when GC declines to fund; it must remain in every downstream CIA template.

### 11.11 Upstream reporting and covenants (operational obligations on Skalar)

- DDR within 14 days of month end; S&M Period Funds Flow ≥ 2 BD before each disbursement.
- Monthly unaudited financials and Cohort Threshold Test calculations ≤ 30 days after month end; audited annuals ≤ 30 days post-audit (explanation if > 240 days).
- Continuous data access: Customer Dataset per Underlying Product (schema per Schedule E), all cohort/RI/threshold/spend data, 6-month pre-closing history.
- Notify GC ≤ 3 BD of: recipient new indebtedness, recipient fundraising plans, recipient material adverse effects, recipient sale/restructuring, any Senior Loan Default. Disclose any Skalar indebtedness > $10M aggregate within 7 days.
- No liens on Invested Receivables; no competing receivable financings; new Skalar Indebtedness requires an intercreditor/subordination agreement approved by GC (GC must negotiate in good faith consistent with precedent).
- Downstream amendments to any Invested Receivable beyond min(2.5% of PFA, $25,000) require GC consent.
- Anti-cash-hoarding: subsidiaries upstream RI before payments fall due.
- Information rights (Exhibit D): quarterly access to CEO/CFO/COO/SVP Marketing, board member access quarterly, annual inspection, VCOC management rights. No GC control over Skalar's direction of Actual S&M Spend.

## 12. Back-to-back credit-line management (operating policy)

Purpose: keep Skalar permanently capable of meeting upstream obligations out of downstream rights. Skalar's structural position: committed lender downstream, uncommitted borrower upstream, guarantor of portfolio collections on an accrual basis, with an upstream cap that accrues in time (16%/yr → 2.0x) against downstream caps frozen at payback.

### 12.1 Asymmetry matrix

| Dimension | Upstream (owed to GC) | Downstream (owed to Skalar) | Managed by |
|---|---|---|---|
| Commitment | Discretionary | Mandatory within caps | 12.2(1), Capital Disruption clause downstream |
| Income basis | Accrual, greater-of | Cash collections | 12.2(3) |
| Settlement | ~day 17 post month | Template-dependent (often ~day 60) | 12.2(2) |
| Cap | min(2.0x, 16% XIRR), time-accruing | MOIC 1.08–1.6, fixed at payback | 12.2(4) |
| Breach sharing | 100% to GC, tests set per-IR | 100% to Skalar, tests fixed in Schedule A | 12.3(1) |
| Acceleration | 3 BD | 5 BD | 12.3(2) |
| Wind-down trigger | Recipient MAC, >5% of vintage trailing-3M RI | Company-initiated cancellations >5% (policy carve-out) | 12.3(3) |
| Default rate | 16% | 18% | spread covers late-pay carry |

### 12.2 Cash-flow invariants

1. **Solvency without GC.** Plan every month at `gc_funded(t) = 0`: `pool(t)` ≥ next-month committed downstream funding + current-month upstream remittances + opex. GC lending is upside, never a dependency. Downstream Capital Disruption clauses are the only relief valve and must exist in every CIA template.
2. **Net at the IR.** Exercise downstream §2.1(c) netting every period: reduce the next Investment Amount by sharing owed from the prior period. This converts the day-60 receivable into reduced day-0 outflow and pre-funds the day-17 upstream remittance independently of collections. Future CIA templates: `calc_window <= 14d`, `settlement_window <= 3d` to mirror upstream natively.
3. **Greater-of containment.** Upstream obligations are computed on amounts *due* downstream. Controls: identical Customer Dataset definitions up and down; Collections Account + early DACA in every downstream deal; downstream default rate ≥ upstream + 2%; pursue 18% default interest on any downstream slippage from day one.
4. **Per-vintage XIRR ledger.** Maintain dated-cashflow ledgers per vintage; remit exactly `min(gc_sharing_pct × RI_due, remaining gc_cap)` — both legs evaluated at each calculation date. Over-remittance is unrecoverable; under-remittance accrues 16% default interest.
5. **Velocity preference.** Downstream MOIC 1.08 inside 4 months annualizes well above the 16% upstream accrual; the marginal +0.014/month (~16.8%/yr) barely covers it. Capital allocation favors fast-payback cohorts; slow cohorts earn Skalar ~nothing on the GC-funded 95%.
6. **Leverage efficiency.** Request the `gc_funding_pct` ceiling (0.95) on every vintage; fund the residual 0.05 from the pool. Restore the 0.95:0.05 proportion through Funding Adjustments after every spend reconciliation. Any vintage funded below ceiling consumes pool capital at degraded leverage and requires explicit justification.

### 12.3 Governance invariants

1. **Threshold subordination.** Before countersigning any upstream IR: verify the proposed GC Cohort Threshold Test is no tighter than every active downstream test at equivalent ages. Never accept an upstream test Skalar's portfolio cannot demonstrably pass from current data.
2. **Acceleration buffer.** Upstream demand is 3 BD; downstream extraction is 5 BD. Hold liquid reserves ≥ the largest single-vintage Outstanding Investor Share × wound-down stress %, covering the 2-day gap. New downstream CIAs: ≤ 3 BD demand windows.
3. **Wind-down gap.** Recipient insolvency obligates Skalar upstream (11.7) while the downstream claim is accelerating in parallel (9.1). Treat `wound_down_pct × outstanding_gc_cap` per recipient as a contingent liability; reserve against recipients with deteriorating threshold trajectories before MAC declaration.
4. **Notice pass-through.** Downstream notice rights (3–5 BD) feed upstream notice duties (3 BD). Same-day forwarding policy; no discretionary holding of recipient bad news.
5. **Consent chain.** Downstream receivable amendments > min(2.5%, $25k) already require Skalar consent; the same threshold requires GC consent upstream. Route both approvals together.

### 12.4 Monitoring registers (agent-maintainable)

- Vintage register: `tau, PFA, funding_date, gc_funding_pct, dated remittances, cum_remit, xirr16_amount, 2x ceiling, binding leg, projected closure`.
- Recipient register: `recipient, trailing-3M RI share per vintage, threshold trajectory vs downstream and upstream tests, distance to graduation triggers ($2.5M period spend / $20M outstanding), MAC indicators`.
- Liquidity register: `month, pool balance, committed downstream funding, upstream remittances due (accrual), netting offsets available, gc_funded requested vs granted`.

## 13. Validation invariants (agent checklist)

1. `S~(d,k,i) >= 0` and `sum_k S~(d,k,i) <= return_cap(d,i)` for every cohort.
2. `S~(d,k,i) = 0` for all `k > k*(d,i)` and for all `k` past `max_cohort_life`.
3. Effective sharing rate = the rate Skalar requests at reconciliation, defaulting to the negotiated `sharing_pct(d)`; absent an election, adjustments restore the negotiated proportion (no Scenario I/II taxonomy).
4. Underspend implies `A > 0` and is always settled (never silently dropped); failure to process an FA does not waive amounts owed.
5. Cohort membership: every customer maps to exactly one cohort; `first_period` computed pre-filtering; customers with `first_period < 0` excluded from all funded cohorts.
6. Collections may be negative in a period; Reference Income and sharing follow sign; `S~` floor at 0 via the truncation formula.
7. Aggregations join on calendar month, never on local period index.
8. `funding_need(t)` is evaluated at `t` for periods starting `t+1` (cash staged one month ahead).
9. Per deal: cumulative funded `<= commitment_amount`; per period `F <= max_period_funding` and growth-capped; one IR per period.
10. Vintage attribution: every customer maps to exactly one GC vintage by join month; every dollar of vintage RI decomposes into exactly one `(tau)` claim; remittances never exceed `gc_cap(tau, m) - cum_remit`.
11. All flows USD; non-Business-Day amounts due the preceding Business Day; default interest on 360/30 basis.
12. Upstream remittances computed on amounts **due** (accrual greater-of), never reduced by downstream non-collection.
13. `gc_cap` evaluated with both legs (2.0x and 16% XIRR on dated flows) at every calculation date; binding leg recorded per vintage per month.
14. Before any upstream IR countersign: upstream threshold test ≤ all active downstream tests at equivalent ages.
15. Monthly liquidity plan solvent at `gc_funded(t) = 0`.
16. Fungibility: no formula, claim, or report conditions on the origin (equity vs retained sharing) of Skalar-side dollars. Vintage identity attaches only to GC-funded amounts (`PFA`, dated remittances); `pool(t)` is a single scalar.
17. Proportion invariant per vintage: post-Funding-Adjustment, `PFA(tau) / actual_sm_spend_skalar(tau) = gc_funding_pct = 0.95` and Skalar share `= 0.05`; deviations exist only under a documented GC election, recorded in the vintage register with the FA reference.
18. Threshold engine: every deal (and every upstream IR) carries an explicit mechanic election `{mechanic, timing, exit}`; absent an election, apply the company defaults `{I linear ladder, any_day, breakeven}`. The test basis is always cohort Reference Income over origin-period actual TOTAL S&M spend — never received sharing, never the funded amount alone. Elected mechanic verified against agreement language before execution.
19. Adjustment diligence: every funding adjustment is raised the moment actual spend is determinable, settled standalone outside the settlement calendar, and its GC ratable share (capital advance on the vintage) propagated at once; no adjustment defers to a settlement date or settles twice. Rationale: MOIC pricing makes `F_eff` correction urgent; ~95% of adjustment flows belong to GC.
